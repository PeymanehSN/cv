Current Research Direction

I am developing a research project on behavioral incentives for circular-economy decisions, using a digital wardrobe setting as a field-experiment environment.

The project asks whether people can be moved from passive ownership to sustainable action when unused items are made visible as economic, social, and environmental assets. It studies how valuation, loss framing, social comparison, default recommendations, marketplace signals, and sustainability feedback affect decisions to sell, donate, reuse, recycle, or keep unused fashion items.

The project builds on my doctoral work on incentives, wage transparency, envy, and organizational design, while extending these mechanisms into sustainable consumption and circular-economy behavior.

Research Identity

My doctoral research studied how social comparisons affect contractual and organizational design, particularly when transparency changes how individuals perceive incentives and peer outcomes.

My environmental economics work examined strategic incentives in international environmental agreements. My current work brings these strands together by studying how information and incentives can support sustainable consumption and circular-economy behavior.

Across these projects, my central question is how institutions, organizations, and platforms should be designed when people care not only about material payoffs, but also about fairness, status, comparison, identity, and trust.

Research-Driven Entrepreneurship

My applied work explores how behavioral economics can be translated into products, experiments, and decision tools. I view entrepreneurship not only as company-building, but also as a way to create real-world environments where behavioral mechanisms can be observed and tested.

Current applied project • BAVO

Digital wardrobe setting for circular-economy behavior

BAVO is a product concept designed to make the hidden economic and sustainability value of wardrobes visible. It can support field experiments on valuation, loss framing, peer comparison, default recommendations, resale, reuse, donation, and recycling decisions.

Previous applied project • MindMeld Money
