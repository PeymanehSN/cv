Current research direction

From Dormant Wardrobes to Circular Action

I am developing a theory-guided field experiment on circular-economy decisions, using a digital wardrobe setting to study how behavioral interventions change what people do with unused possessions.

The project asks whether people can be moved from passive ownership to sustainable action when unused items are made visible as economic, social, and environmental assets. It would test how digital valuation, loss framing, peer comparison, default recommendations, marketplace signals, and sustainability feedback affect decisions to sell, donate, reuse, recycle, or keep unused fashion items.

This project builds on my doctoral research on wage transparency, envy, incentive contracts, and organizational design, while extending these mechanisms into sustainable consumption and circular-economy behavior.

Research identity

What Connects My Work

My doctoral research studied how social comparisons affect contractual and organizational design, especially when transparency changes how individuals interpret incentives and peer outcomes.

My environmental economics work examined strategic incentives in international environmental agreements. My current direction connects these strands by asking how incentive design and behavioral interventions can support sustainable consumption.

Across my work, the central question is how organizations, institutions, and platforms should be designed when people care not only about payoffs, but also about fairness, status, comparison, identity, and trust.

Applied work

Research-Driven Entrepreneurship

I view entrepreneurship as a way to create real-world environments where behavioral mechanisms can be observed, tested, and translated into useful products.

BAVO

Digital wardrobe as experimental setting

BAVO is a product concept designed to make the hidden economic and sustainability value of wardrobes visible. It can support field experiments on valuation, loss framing, peer comparison, default recommendations, resale, reuse, donation, and recycling decisions.

MindMeld Money

Behavioral finance and decision-making

A fintech concept built around behavioral economics, game theory, and gamified simulations to study financial decisions and support more reflective user choices.
